# Kinship Manifesto & User Bill of Rights

## Preamble

In a world where digital connection has been commodified, where personal data has become a currency, and where algorithms dictate human interaction, a new framework is required. We stand at the crossroads of intimate community building and digital sovereignty, affirming a universal principle: human connection must not come at the cost of personal privacy or individual autonomy.

Kinship is built on the uncompromising belief that technology should facilitate real-world ecosystems—chosen families, polycules, cooperatives, and local communities—without extracting their value. We reject the standard model of surveillance capitalism and algorithmic feeds. Instead, we are building a secure, non-hierarchical infrastructure that treats social energy, time, and collective resources as a communal ecosystem that requires nurturing and absolute protection.

## Core Principles

**1. Privacy by Default, Encryption by Design**
Confidentiality must always be maintained. All data objects, communications, and logistical planning within Kinship utilize End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) by default. Private keys are held solely by the data owners and their intended recipients, ensuring that no host, server provider, or developer can ever access plaintext content. Encryption is not a luxury; it is freedom expressed as technology.

**2. Relational Autonomy and "Shells of Trust"**
Mainstream platforms force users into binary categories of "public" or "private." We believe human relationships are fluid and complex. Kinship is built on "shells of trust," granting users the granular ability to share different facets of their lives—time, energy, and resources—with different intersecting circles, preserving individual boundaries without sacrificing collective coordination.

**3. Non-Extractive Community Economics**
The current technological and economic model is extractive—damaging the very social networks and human resources it relies upon. Kinship commits to a non-extractive, regenerative architecture. We will never monetize user behavior, sell data to third parties, or utilize targeted advertising. We build tools that allow communities to generate, track, and retain their own social and financial capital locally.

## The User Bill of Rights

As a user of the Kinship platform, you are guaranteed the following inalienable digital rights:

**I. The Right to Digital Sovereignty**
Every person has the right to control their data, identities, and digital footprints. No authority—public or private—may appropriate, exploit, or manipulate your personal information. You, and your vetted community, are the sole owners of your ecosystem.

**II. The Right to Informed Decision-Making and Control**
You have the right to a clear, transparent user interface that allows you to make informed choices about who sees your data and how it is utilized. The platform must ensure you retain absolute control over the disclosure of your availability, energy levels, and shared resources.

**III. The Right to "Trust Without Exposure"**
You have the right to coordinate with secondary networks (friends of friends, extended community members) without exposing personally identifiable information (PII) or the private details of your primary inner circle.

**IV. The Right to Leave (Data Portability)**
You give your data, and you have the absolute right to take it away. If you choose to leave the platform, you have the right to easily, efficiently, and freely extract all your uploaded information in a usable format. Furthermore, your data will not simply be "disabled"; it will be permanently eliminated from the service's servers.
